## 1. Executive Summary

Safe Hands Staffing Agency Pty Ltd is a NSW-based childcare staffing
agency supplying qualified educators to early learning centres, OSHC
programs, and private families on a casual, relief, short-term, and
emergency-fill basis. We connect compliant Cert III, Diploma, ECT,
room-leader, and inclusion-support educators to centres that face daily
ratio shortfalls, and to families that need flexible, vetted in-home
care.

This document is the founder and operating team's working reference. It
defines what we are building, how we will operate in the first 12
months, the financial reality of a sub-$20K bootstrap launch, and the
milestones we will hold ourselves accountable to.

**Year 1 Operating Goals (NSW)**

- **15 active workers** on the bench by month 12, ramping from 3 at
  launch.

- **8 contracted childcare centres** with active service agreements by
  month 12.

- **$140K–$170K in agency revenue** in Year 1; positive monthly net
  margin from month 4.

- **≥ 95% shift fill rate** and ≤ 4-hour average response time on
  emergency requests.

- **100% compliance** — zero shifts filled by a worker with an expired
  WWCC, First Aid, or Police Check.

**Our Edge**

Three things separate Safe Hands from category competitors. First, a
healthcare and compliance-trained operator running the business — not an
ex-recruiter. Second, a documented compliance backbone (WWCC, HLTAID012,
Police, Anaphylaxis, NDIS Worker Screening) with automated expiry
alerts, so centres never get a non-compliant worker. Third, intentional
capability with special-needs and complex-behaviour children (ASD, ADHD,
behavioural support), which is underserved by generic relief agencies.

**The Capital Plan**

Safe Hands launches on a lean bootstrap of approximately $15K–$18K in
startup capital, sequenced across six weeks of foundational setup. The
model reaches operational break-even at roughly 90 filled shifts per
month and is cash-flow positive once weekly recurring shifts pass that
threshold. Year 1 is operator-led; the first hire (a part-time Booking
Coordinator) is funded out of trading margin in Q3.

## 3. Market Analysis (NSW Focus)

### 3.1 Why Now

NSW operates roughly 5,800 approved early childhood education and care
services under the Education and Care Services National Law. The sector
has run a chronic workforce shortage for several years, with peak
shortfall periods coinciding with school-term starts, illness waves, and
end-of-year burnout. Centres are required by law to meet
educator-to-child ratios (Reg 123) at all times — falling under ratio
risks regulatory action and forces room closures. This is the structural
pain Safe Hands solves.

Three macro drivers reinforce demand: continued growth in dual-income
households, the Federal Government's expanded Child Care Subsidy lifting
demand for centre-based places, and the NDIS continuing to fund
disability-related care for children, opening a higher-margin specialist
segment.

## 4. Service Model

### 4.1 The Two Channels

Safe Hands runs two distinct channels with shared operational
infrastructure. Both are governed by a written agreement (the Childcare
Centre Service Agreement and the Private Family Client Agreement), and
both draw from the same vetted worker bench.

**Channel A — Centres (B2B)**

- Master Service Agreement signed before first booking.

- Booking via phone, SMS, email, or online form.

- Minimum 3-hour shift; cancellation fees per the agreement.

- Weekly invoicing, NET-14 terms.

- Charge-out is per the rate schedule (Section 9 / Appendix A).

**Channel B — Private Families (B2C)**

- Private Family Client Agreement signed before first booking.

- Booking via website form or direct contact.

- Minimum 3-hour shift; $30 admin fee per filled shift.

- Invoice within 24 hours of shift completion; payment within 48 hours.

- No-poaching clause: 12-month direct-hire fee if family hires the
  worker independently.

### 4.2 The Worker Bench

The bench is the asset. Every worker on the bench has cleared the
contractor onboarding checklist (see Appendix B / source document: Child
care worker onboarding mandatory checks): WWCC, Police Check, HLTAID012
First Aid, CPR (annual), Anaphylaxis, Asthma, Cert III or Diploma in
ECEC, identity, right to work, and references. Workers are categorised
by qualification level, suburb, travel radius, weekly availability, and
special-needs / complex-behaviour capability.

### 4.3 The Compliance Backbone

This is what most relief agencies do badly and what we do as a
discipline. Every compliance document has a stored expiry date. The
system flags, at 60 / 30 / 14 / 7 days out, any expiring WWCC, Police
Check, First Aid, CPR, or anaphylaxis training. Workers with a lapsed
clearance are automatically blocked from new shift offers. This is
non-negotiable — a single non-compliant placement can close a centre's
room and end the relationship.

## 6. Operations Plan

### 6.1 Hours of Operation

Booking line operates 6:00am–8:00pm Mon–Fri and 7:00am–6:00pm weekends.
After-hours emergency line for live-shift incidents only. Standard
shifts 7:00am–7:00pm; OSHC standard 6:30am–9:00am and 2:30pm–6:30pm.

### 6.2 Booking Workflow

1.  Centre or family submits booking via web form, phone, or SMS.

2.  Booking captured in scheduling tool; status set to PENDING
    ALLOCATION.

3.  System matches workers on qualification, suburb, availability, and
    any special-needs flags.

4.  Top-3 matched workers are notified; first to accept is allocated.

5.  Confirmation issued to client and worker (with worker name,
    qualification, ETA).

6.  Worker submits timesheet via mobile after shift; client signs
    digitally.

7.  Invoice generated automatically; client pays per terms; worker paid
    weekly.

### 6.3 Recruitment Workflow

8.  Inbound application → 48-hour acknowledgement.

9.  Document collection: WWCC, Police Check (≤12 mo), HLTAID012, CPR,
    Cert III or Diploma, ID (100-pt), right-to-work, two references.

10. Phone screen (15 min): availability, suburb, special-needs comfort,
    scenario questions.

11. Face-to-face or video interview (30 min) — child-safety scenario;
    behaviour-management response.

12. Reference checks (two minimum, both reachable).

13. Code of Conduct + Mandatory Reporting acknowledgement signed.

14. Bank, super, and TFN forms; insurance status confirmed (contractor
    or employee).

15. First-shift buddy or shadow if available; otherwise post-shift
    debrief required.

### 8.5 Break-even

Operating break-even is the point where monthly margin covers monthly
fixed cost. Fixed monthly opex (insurance, software, phone, marketing,
sundry) at lean = ~$2,700. Average margin per filled shift (rate
margin + admin fee) = ~$92. Break-even = ~30 filled shifts per month,
achievable with 4 active workers each doing 2 shifts a week. Comfortable
break-even with founder allowance included = ~90 filled shifts per
month, expected by month 5.

## 10. Compliance Framework

Compliance is the product. Every artefact below is owned,
version-controlled, and reviewed annually.

### 10.1 Regulatory Framework We Operate Under

- **Education and Care Services National Law (NSW):** Sets ratios,
  qualifications, child-safety obligations.

- **Education and Care Services National Regulations:** Reg 123
  (ratios), Reg 126–128 (qualifications), Reg 136 (first aid).

- **National Quality Framework (NQF) + National Quality Standard:**
  Service quality benchmarks.

- **NSW Child Safe Standards (Office of the Children's Guardian):** 10
  standards, mandatory.

- **Working With Children Check (NSW) legislation:** WWCC required for
  all educators.

- **Fair Work Act 2009 + Children's Services Award 2020:** Worker pay
  and entitlements.

- **Privacy Act 1988 (Cth):** Worker, child, and family data handling.

- **WHS Act 2011 (NSW):** Workplace safety obligations to placed
  workers.

- **NDIS Practice Standards (Phase 2):** Once registered as NDIS
  Provider.

## 11. Implementation Roadmap

Anchored to the existing 6-Week Start-Up Plan (see source: Copy of Safe
hands 6 week start up.pdf), then extended to a 24-month milestone plan.

### 11.1 Weeks 1–6 — Foundations

- **Week 1:** Pty Ltd registration; ABN; business name; domain; bank
  account; service-area definition.

- **Week 2:** Insurances bound (PL, PI, WC); core policies finalised;
  payroll/accounting set up.

- **Week 3:** Branding live; website + booking form; scheduling tool
  configured; e-sign templates.

- **Week 4:** First centre outreach (target: 20 directors contacted);
  first 3 worker applications processed.

- **Week 5:** First MSA signed (target); 5 workers fully onboarded and
  bench-ready.

- **Week 6:** First filled shift live; post-shift debrief loop
  established; KPI dashboard wired.

### 11.2 Months 3–6 — Trade & Tighten

- Hit 5–8 active workers, 90+ filled shifts/month.

- Sign 3–5 centre MSAs; 5–10 active families.

- Tighten SOPs from real friction; document every recurring issue.

- Establish RTO/TAFE recruitment pipeline.

### 11.3 Months 7–12 — Scale Y1

- Hit 12–15 active workers, 180+ filled shifts/month.

- Hire part-time Booking Coordinator (month 7–9).

- Soft-launch private channel marketing (Meta + parenting groups).

- Y1 financial review; tax planning with CPA.

### 11.4 Months 13–18 — Phase 2 (NDIS + scale)

- Begin NDIS Provider registration (3–4 month process).

- Hire Recruitment Coordinator + Compliance Lead.

- Pilot custom platform build (Python / FastAPI / SQL Server / Azure) —
  see Tech Roadmap (Appendix C).

- Target 30+ active workers, 4–5 active centres in regional NSW.

### 11.5 Months 19–24 — Multi-state preparation

- Operations Manager hire.

- VIC market entry feasibility (Service Victoria WWC, CPSU regs).

- Custom platform live; SaaS booking tool retired.

- Year 3 forecast: 60+ workers, 13K+ filled shifts.

**Appendix C — Tech Roadmap (Phase 2 Custom Build)**

Once revenue and operational scale support the build, the agency moves
from configured SaaS to a purpose-built platform. Indicative
architecture, owned by the founder's technical co-build:

- **Backend:** Python (FastAPI), pydantic; async I/O for booking
  matching.

- **Database:** SQL Server (Azure SQL) for transactional + compliance
  data; Cosmos DB for unstructured worker notes / event logs.

- **Compliance engine:** Scheduled jobs to evaluate every clearance
  expiry; auto-block bookings on lapse; generate weekly compliance
  digest.

- **Matching engine:** Rule-based first (qualification, suburb,
  availability, special-needs flags), with ML re-ranking once
  shift-completion data is available.

- **Frontend:** Worker mobile-first PWA, client portal, internal
  dispatcher dashboard.

- **Hosting:** Azure App Service + Azure SQL + Azure Functions for
  scheduled jobs; Azure AD B2C for identity.

- **AI assist (Phase 2.5):** LLM-driven intake parsing for inbound
  centre booking emails; anomaly detection on compliance and incident
  reporting trends.
